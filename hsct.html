HSCT
Haematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation is a specific treatment scheme for Autoimmune diseases like Multiple Sclerosis where is used immunosuppresive chemotherapy with a combination of own (autologous) stem cell reinfusion, to ‘’ reboot’’ the immune system.

STEP 0
Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation - (sometimes referred to as AHSCT) is one of the newest and most promising method of treatment that can effectively stop progression and save the quality of life for a patients with different autoimmune diseases.
Treatment is similar to the chemotherapy as for oncological and haematological diseases like blood cancers, where high dose immunosuppressive therapy is used for removing self-reactive cells and inducing a reboot of the immune system, thereby controlling disease.
But high-dose treatment destroys the stem cells in your bone marrow as well as the disease – causing cells. Thats why, patient’s own (Autologus) stem cells should be collected and stored at laboratory before chemotherapy administered, until they are required.
After the treatment, stem cells are reinfused back to your blood through a drip. New immune system starts to grow, which hopefully will no longer attack the body.
In case of Multiple sclerosis the immune system mistakenly attacks the protective layer (myelin) around nerves. After patients undergoes AHSCT treatment, - process is halted, the immune system more self – tolerant, and no longer attack your myelin.
AHSCT is a one-time procedure which involves several steps, including:
1. Stem cell stimulation for their further collection from peripheral bloodsteam.
2. Collection , separration and cryopreservation at -180 until cells are required.
3. Immune system elimination with high dose chemotherapy treatment (Conditioning).
4. Own (autologous) stem cell reinfusion back to the patient. (Transplantation)

STEP 1
Medical examination
The preparatory stage, which involves taking a careful anamnesis, making a complete clinical picture of the disease and an extensive medical examination of the patient, which includes:
Medical history collection;
MRI (brain, cervical, thoracic);
Chest X-ray, ECG, Sonography
Duration: 1-3 days
STEP 2
Mobilization
We use a combination of medications to stimulate the movement of blood cells from bone marrow into the peripheral bloodstream, where they can be gathered. For mobilization we use:
Dropper for infusion;
The chemotherapy drug - Cyclophosphamide
2 injections per day Granulocyte-colony stimulating factor.
Duration: 4 -5 Days
STEP 3
Apheresis
Obtaining stem cells (CD34+) from the peripheral bloodstream using an automated separator of blood components (Spectra Optia apheresis system), which will be freezed and stored at -180°C using liquid nitrogen on demand and will be returned to the patient's body later. The procedure looks like this.
The patient's blood is gathered through a needle in the arm;
Stem cell separation takes place;
Cryopreservation
Duration: 1 -2 Days
STEP 4
Conditioning
The main stage of treatment in which the patient undergoes a high dose of chemotherapy to suppress autoreactive cells to remove completely or partially the immune system (HDIT), instead of which a new one will be created later when the stem cells are reinfused.
The conditioning modes which are used in personalized treatment protocol:
Cyclophosphamide
ATG (Anti-thymocyte globulin);
Fludarabine;
Rituximab;
Intrathecal for some immunological drugs;
Concomitant therapy at all stages of the procedure.
Duration: 4 Days
Day 0
STEP 5
Transplantation
Re-infusing stem cells to the patient for the purpose of rapid hematological recovery and reduction of the period of cytopenia. Returned CD34+ stem cells also helps to inactivate remaining autoreactive cells at peripheral blood stream and tissue. As a result, the process of reloading the immune system is launched, which allows:
Saving quality of life for patient’s with long term autoimmune diseases;
Prevent disability worsening;
Preserve quality of life at the highest possible level.
Duration: 15-20 Minutes
STEP 6
Recovery and isolation
The beginning of the neutropenic period, which is characterized by a gradual recovery of the immune system, but still low levels of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell responsible for infections. The patient is strictly forbidden to leave the ward and it is important to pass additionally:
ATG (anti-thymocyte globulin) immunotherapy on days +1 to +3 for additional T-cell suppression;
Rituximab injection for additional B-cell suppression
Antiviral, antibacterial, antifungal therapy as covering therapy for cytopenia;
Supporting therapy with blood components – colony-stimulating factors, which can reduce the period of cytopenia by 5-7 days.
Duration: 2 Weeks
STEP 7
Discharge
Patient is returning home after complete recovery of hematopoiesis. Our team monitors carefully the patient's blood tests, body temperature and heart rate for whole 30 days of treatment . Active inflammation and progression of disease stops after treatment and patient is starting rehabilitation process.
You will have a final meeting with prof. Polushin , where will be discussed:
Further supportive therapy
Public prohibitions
Food recommendations
Post transplantation psychological considerations
(At meeting full list of recommendations will be given you with a translation at any language is needed ).
Duration: 1-3 days

Before HSCT

To start a consultation about HSCT treatment with our AHSCT1 center specialists, patients should have a confirmed positive diagnosis of Multiple Sclerosis or any other autoimunne disorder what is listed on our Treatments page.

As a member of European Society for Blood and Marrow Transplantation, we follow up their refered guidelines for eligibility criteria. But there can be exlcusions what we observe individually and make a final conclusion - accepted or not, only after detailed examination.

Patients diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis EDSS score up to 7.0 can be accepted for transplantation.
We do not have any specific age restriction, if your individual health status has a stable organ functioning without any contrindicating factors.
Patient should not have any chronic or virus infections, Negative - HIV, HBV, HCV.
Patient must provide the latest MRI. It can be uploaded at Application form page or sent us by email.
Patient has to be able to travel and remain at Saint Petersburg, Russia during a 28 - 32 day period, accompanied by a caregiver if it s possible.
If at arrival, after passing our medical examinations, will be not accepted for HSCT, patient will be refunded.
